Feedback, Answers & How To

Inside of the graphical art, we have many different kind of artists and people. All whom have their own traits, ideas, goals and hopes for their artwork. However! There are several things which counts for all of us, and there are several things that define an artist. The main thing that defines and artist is the people that view their art and themselves. So I will be talking about both of these in this thread, more specifically I will be talking about Feedback, Replies/Answers & How To do both of these. So please bare with me here.

Feedback
Feedback belongs to the viewer, even though an artist may be one of these from time to time. Mainly why artists ( me included ) ask for feedback from people is because we want another person's point of view on our work. ( In the sense that another pair of eyes may witness what the other don't ) So we post our work, and then we wait, exited for some helpful feedback. Now I am the kind of person that thinks that any information is useful in some form or another, however I don't find most of the feedback I read from people to others or to be that helpful. Mainly because they lack substance and thus they aren't useful and may end up hurting the artist's point of view on their art or community. What I am referring to are comments such as;

" It looks amazing! "
" I really like it, don't know why. I just REALLY like it! "

Now, I will say that comments such as these are really nice to get and it may make most artists really happy. However, they aren't helpful in any way at all. As I've said, most artists post their art in order to improve and they won't improve with comments like these. So let me get to the point on how you make some good feedback and how you should build up your feedback comment on a graphical thread.


How To: Make a Feedback Comment!

First of all, you should study and take a close look at the image that has been posted by the artist. Make up an opinion if you like it or not and then find out why you like or dislike it. Now why do you need to do this? Well, as most people aren't professional's when it comes to graphical work, they are still able to post some feedback. You see, art is very subjective and a single piece of art can mean a million things. So, why do you need to find out why you like the artwork? Well that is rather obvious really, as you need to do that in order to tell the artist what you thinks good and what you think is bad with their artwork. Here are an example on a "bad" feedback comment and a "good" one as well:

Good Comment

It looks really good, the colors really mix good however you may want to weaken the colors a little bit. They are a little bit strong on my eyes. The size is a little big too, its a little hard to get a whole view of the picture on my screen.

Bad Comment

I like it, it looks good and the quality of it is great!


You noticed the difference in these comments? Well, if you didn't let me point them out for you.The first comment which I have ranked as good gives several things. First of all, its a good comment because it isn't harassing the artist in any way. ( Making insults, hating etc ) It also gives the artist information on whats good about his/hers art aswell as whats not so good about it. Another thing it does is that it never states that anything is wrong! Here's a thing, nothing in art is wrong. Nothing at all, everything is fully correct. Want to know why that is? Well its because of this:

Art isn't wrong, its subjective, its a way to express yourself

As that says, no art can be directly wrong. So saying that something is wrong in your feedback comment is a rather obvious NO! Now from the "bad" comment, I've pointed out the things the other one did good, so the "bad" one did the opposite of those. It had no information to what was good and what was "bad" about the artist's work. However, it did not harass the artist in anyway either. The reason why I did this is to show everyone that even though someone says good things about someone in a feedback comment, doesn't make the said comment "good".


Replies/Answers
Now, this is where the artist is the main point. What I mean with this header is how and why an artist may respond to feedback given to him/her. Now, this is a point which me and several other artists have gotten a decent amount of hate from. Mainly because we comment back that we don't fully agree with the said comment about our work. ( This is another point to that art is subjective ) I get why people annoyed that an artist dislike their feedback and they label it as the artist isn't able to handle feedback properly. This really isn't the case either, let me explain a little.

When an artist gets a feedback comment which I have labeled as "bad" and they comment on it saying that the feedback is "incorrect" or in other words not "good", they're labeled as people who cannot take feedback. That is really false and just shows how much a standard community has fallen over the past years which forums has been a growing subject. On to the topic at hand again though. I will explain how to spot a person who cannot take feedback, and how to spot one that just disagree with a feedback. ( As art is subjective )


Person who disagrees

Thanks for the comment, though it doesn't really give me any information at all. Could you please elaborate a little? ( As this kind of feedback which you've given me are pretty pointless )

Person who cannot take feedback

No, you're wrong. Its not like that, you just don't have any idea what you're talking about. You think you can do better?


I deeply hope that you managed to see the difference here. The first comment states several points. The most obvious one is that he/she says that the comment is pointless, and because of this people tend to assume that they cannot take feedback. But stop and think a little, the comment may be completely pointless ( as I showed earlier in this thread ) so they are entitled to say that. Now, how do you spot that they are able to take feedback, they just didn't like your specific comment? Well its easy. From my example, you can see that the person also asks for a more detailed version of the same comment. In other words, they ask for more feedback. Now if they weren't able to handle that or your don't want that, then they wouldn't be asking for more.

Now take a look at the second example. In this example you can read that the artist doesn't only neglect the feedback given. But more importantly, the artist calls the feedback directly wrong and then insults the person given the feedback. This shows that the person has gone defensive, this proves that the artist isn't able to mentally handle the fact that someone disagree's with him. This does on another note, not make the artist wrong at all. It also doesn't make the person commenting wrong either as they are both correct. ( Art is subjective )


How To: Answer on Comments!

The very first thing you should remember, is to never ever insult anyone. ( That counts at all times, be it in any situation ) Don't try to lower someone because they gave some feedback that was pointed negatively towards you or your work. The reason why you are to avoid this at all times is because it makes a conversation go hostile rather quickly and it may ruin for both parts. The next thing to avoid is to state a personal opinion, as it may sound as if you neglect any other opinion. However you can say that you disagree with certain things, or say that a certain thing was meant to be that way if someone comment on it as a bad thing. ( For example the lightning or color scheme ) The next thing you want to avoid is blaming someone or something else, like saying that the artwork was requested by someone and the reason it is "bad" is because it is how the requester wanted. Or to say that you rushed it, this can be taken as if you are hiding and trying to avoid any negative reputation around you and your art. ( Though it actually makes it worse )

What you can do however, is to ask for a more detailed feedback comment and you can say that you disagree with certain parts of it. As you are entitled to your own opinion, as so is everyone else. Respect their opinion, and they will respect yours.
