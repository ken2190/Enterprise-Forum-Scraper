RE: CMS or starting from scratch? 07-21-2014, 09:35 PM #13
(07-21-2014, 08:52 PM)lady_godiva Wrote: there are other languages that are becoming more and more popular and offer much better functionality. It's just a matter of time before the whole industry turns to these languages.

To give you an example, Ruby on Rails
Ruby (On rails) has been touted as being the next big thing in Web Development by niche coders - for at least the last 10 years. I remember this because I learned the basics of Ruby like a decade ago because even then people were saying how great it was and how it was the future.

And in quite honestly, it still hasnt garnered much more market share since then. It currently ranks below both Python AND Perl on TIOBE's market share index at position 14 (out of 20). Hell Python - in the last 10 years, has gone from a relatively obscure language to surpassing ruby, and even Perl substantially in both scripting and web development. But the number of sites actually designed in Python are still a very small percentage compared to PHP. At this rate of growth it will take a hundred years to replace PHP

So while there are "better" languages - that doesnt mean you should drop PHP because it is not going anywhere in the next decade. You can complement it with Python but if you want real job prospects in web development, PHP is gonna be it.

RE: CMS or starting from scratch? 07-22-2014, 06:40 PM #15
I currently use Wordpress for client work, mainly for local business owners or photographers who want something simplistic, fast, & easy to manage. Wordpress is a great CMS for those types, besides, I wrote my own "plugin" to brand my bashboard to their preference.

However, I'm currently working on creating my own open-source CMS for personal use & distribution & I've gotta tell you, its the way to go. Say you use Wordpress, you want a feature that can't be implemented with a plugin without it opening a vulnerability ..well if you create your on, you can completely monitor your security because the code is your own.

I have about 7 hours invested in the CMS so far & its just about ready for preview release. With a little research you should be able to accomplish it in no time.

I've found this tutorial that may help you, its very in-depth as far as explaining the process, however, the author obviously assumes the reader already knows PHP, so I'd do some research on PHP first.
