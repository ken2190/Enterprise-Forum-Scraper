Also, after some surreal and abstract drawings, I made another piece, where I experimented with... Dadaism!

This style emerged during WW1 and it basically creates an art in an absurd way, to point to the absurdity of the war and killing (at the time the style emerged) and that's what I used: absurdity!

I (virtually) tore all existing strips from one webcomic into little pieces and then assembled a new drawing from them! That's right, following picture contains pieces from all 467 strips created to the date, except for one (that has special meaning to me)! (that's 466 strips technically). The picture is basically sum of the whole history of that comic in one single weird picture!

Thanks ^_^'
Though I would never create art or anything that will others most certainly like, I even dislike most people who target their work to get highest popularity, because it's quite demeaning to the art itself. I think that it should be rich and diverse and come from your own mind, not from what you think that is in other people's minds. If I made such art, I would be unhappy and wouldn't enjoy making it, even if it was much more popular than what I do now.
I prefer much more individuality of the art, rather than going with popular mainstream, because I do it for the art itself, to create something new and unique and also for fun and doing what I like, not for popularity.
And while much less people might identify with what I do, these who do, will be much closer - on the "similar wavelength" if you know what I mean. What's the point of having my art appreciated when I don't appreciate it myself (I'm not saying that it's perfect, it never is and there's always tons of flaws, but that doesn't mean that I can't appreciate it myself, that it's not a complete failure :lol: )
