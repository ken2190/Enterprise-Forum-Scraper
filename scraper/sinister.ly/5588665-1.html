Cheap Portable Retro Gaming 05-24-2017, 10:20 PM #1
This for those of you who miss the old consoles like ps1, dreamcast or any arcade system
I came across a cheap fruitboard, the "Orange Pi"(pretty much a raspberry pi knockoff) for around $33.
This comes with a 16gb class 10 SD Storage, a plastic case and power supply
CLICK HERE FOR LISTING


Picked up this little bargain for myself to try out....Tried the recommended software from the orangepi website but it sucked...

Luckily another team of developers picked up a project called  Retroorangepi which is basically an image with Retropi set up out of the box but for an orange pi
This has HDMI out , controller config and all that good stuff to play with friends on the go

EDIT : I also suggest adding these to keep things cool.

Hope someone out there finds this as entertaining as i do feel free to ask questions


Small side note: This can also be used for various other projects, i found this particular one interesting

Hm, nice little setup.

I run a Raspberry Pi 3 myself but do about the same thing - add a case, some nice controllers (Rock Candy wired PS3 controllers at the moment) and cables so I can do HDMI or Composite to the board and it works well anyways. Hell, it works better than the school computers in about every way for everything so occasionally I'll just use the little Pi as it has Raspbian and all that under the EmulationStation hood.

Spoiler: Image

I keep this one in the school band room - we have good fun with everything from Odyssey to Dreamcast - and since it didn't cost a ton I'm not too worried about the rig. Everyone from 5th to 12th grade gets to mess with it anyways, and I haven't had issue. This image is from today, we had a little MK2 tournament on the thing which was sweet.

I have to ask, how is emulation on the Orange Pi? They looked like cool options back when the Pi 3 wasn't like $50 after shipping, but I didn't look all too hard into the thing. The Pi 3 runs up to some of the earlier/more optomized DC titles and a chunk of the N64 anyways - the only issue I have is that X68000 emulation is very "eh" so I have to lug my 68030 Compact if I want to mess with the things outside home.

Neat thread - I love old systems so eh, this kind of stuff is always cool.
