RE: Do Gaming Companies Listen? 08-11-2016, 05:09 PM #3
Gaming companies are just like any other company out there in the world. Smaller, 'indie' developers tend to listen to player feedback more than the monolithic companies because they have more at stake, so it is in their best interest to create a game which will appeal most to an audience with the greatest chance of that audience giving them sweet moniez $$

The larger a company grows, the less feedback they have to listen to. Some large companies obviously will listen to feedback, but some won't. Blizzard is a great example of this.
If Blizzard is releasing a new game, they usually listen to feedback and change the game accordingly. But as time goes on, they stop giving a shit, because they have already established a loyal playerbase. Blizzard knows they can leach money from that playerbase. evident with StarCraft, WoW etc..

Just like comcast knows they don't have to give a shit about their customers because they already have a monopoly on a certain area.
