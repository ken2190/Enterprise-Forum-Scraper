Feedback is feedback, and constructive feedback is constructive feedback. They have one major difference though, one is useful and the other is not. Opinions depends on each individual, which is what we call an subjective view. Objective views are what is used in constructive feedback, what is objectively bad or wrong. If it falls under this category then the person who points out should be able to explain why without using their own subjective view as a source.

And swallow my pride? Is that the right term to use here? (I honestly don't know.) I posted this to gain a chance to improve on it, not hear people's bland complaints. That doesn't interest me, so subjective meaning doesn't interest me. Objective views interests me, as that is what brings fourth improvement. Saying wrong things is wrong, no matter how you look at it. He stated that I should use another tool to gain the exact same look as I had, now where is the improvement in that? Oh right, nowhere to be seen.

I have no "pride" to speak of, as I am never happy with anything I make. People tend to say that I "act" as if I am not, only to get praise. But I have no interest in what others thing of me, be it good or ill. I was and am after improvement, not nagging.
