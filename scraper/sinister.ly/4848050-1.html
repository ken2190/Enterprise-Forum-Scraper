Win PP Chargebacks as a seller 10-21-2013, 05:38 PM #1
Quote:We all knew that Paypal is totally shit to sellers and they give more protection only to the buyers.

I had been really tired of seeing many chargebacks in this past 1 year. I had lost many disputes as unauthorized. I haven't won even a single thing. I had lost more than 700$ by unauthorized chargeback. So here is a safe tip how to win those kind of things and how to find up fraudsters.

This can be applied for both Personal Payments and Goods/Service payment. Paypal's latest Policy update is that when you sell an item or Tangible goods, if you ship it to the Paypal account holder's address and show shipping proof, no matter what you will win the dispute.

But what can we do for Intangible Items. Let us say LEETKING/COMPLEXITY (I am in total rage while typing these fuckers' names) wants to buy an Intangible goods from you, let us say a VBA/VCC or an ebook or whatever. You should deliver the intangible goods only to the email address where the payment came from (But make sure to tell this to your buyers before they send payment).

When someone sends payment, you will get an email from Paypal that a payment is received. You have to directly reply to that email. Replying directly to that email will go to the buyer's paypal email inbox. So When he makes a dispute as Item not received or Unauthorized payment, you will win the dispute if you show the screenshot that you emailed them with Goods. I tried that in last 2 cases and I won.

Guys who are paying from hacked paypal won't be having the access to the
Paypal email id. So if you say that you will deliver the goods only to their email address, they will say this and that and won't accept at all. Just stop dealing with them and go for the next person. Even if he says he will pay you $2 - $5 extra don't accept the trade and don't do business with him. If you look for $2 profit you will lose everything. I am telling this from my very own Experience.

What if you are offering some kinda services. Let's say, PPD Download service or any Exchange service or Giving followers to fb twitter etc., this is quite real complicated but for our safety we need to do this for sure. Before receiving payment, ask them to send you an email from their Paypal email id to your paypal email id. You can use the following template.

Dear Seller,

I, (Buyers PP Name), want to buy XXXX Service from you. I agree to your TOS that you offer No Refunds/Replacements (if you don't offer then he should mention that for sure) and I would not make any chargeback including Item Not received, Non - Receipt payments or as unauthorized payments that will leave us into any kind of sour business in future. I assure you that this is my primary paypal that I am going to send the money from. Looking forward to have more business with you.

Regards,
Buyers PP Name.

First he should email you something like this. Then ask him to send payment. Once he sent the payment go to the Transaction history and check both Paypal id and his email id (the one he mailed you) is same. If it is different and he says that he has alternate paypal id's like secondary id's or so on, then just refund the payment back to him and don't accept his payment unless he sends from the id where he sent the email from. Again, Paypal email id and his general email id should match up.

This way you can avoid unauthorized dispute for doing services.

Legit guys who really don't wanna scam you will say whatever you want if he really needs that service. There are many trusted members around here. There are staffs, there are admins. Just if they don't wanna do what you say. Even if you miss the sale, no problem at all. Atleast you would be safe from chargeback.

Finally the only way to win all kinda disputes in Virtual Goods/Services is by means of Receiving email from the buyer.

But taking these as your advantage and if you don't deliver what you are ought to, then you would be the BIGGEST DUMBASS FAGGOT.

Thanks for Reading this Patiently.
